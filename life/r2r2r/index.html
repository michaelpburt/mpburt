the grand canyon

rim to rim to rim

november 7th, 2015

Points west



The rim to rim to rim is a hike that starts on the South Rim of the Grand Canyon, crosses over the Colorado River and up to the North Rim, and then comes all the way back to the South Rim. It covers about 48 miles and 10,000ft of elevation gain. It is typically completed as an ultra-marathon. I threw out the idea of doing the rim to rim to rim to a few friends throughout the summer. Most thought I was a little nuts, most of all Janna. Well fast forward to October and she is the one convincing me to do it. I was a little nervous about all that hiking, but reluctantly agreed. I had done almost 20 hikes over the summer and I thought I was up to the challenge.


Nither of us was really committed to a 6-8 week training regime, so we just kind of said fuck it. I went on a couple trail runs over the weeks leading up to the hike but not much more than that. As the date grew closer, I got more and more nervous about the distance. It peaked right as we began our hike. But before we could do that, we had to drive about 700 miles!


We left Denver around 3:30AM on Friday morning. the Front Range was in the middle of an early season snow storm and Vail Pass was pretty socked in at 6:00AM. By the time we made it to Grand Junction, it was all sun. The drive through Moab was beautiful. I had never driven through Utah and I was really looking forward to it.


We made it to Grand Canyon National Park before sunset and were rewarded with some beautiful vistas of the big ditch catching the last rays of the day. We went to bed early and woke up around 1:00AM to start our big day.


The hike



After cramming in some last minute snacks, We hit the trail at 2:30AM. We tried to carry about 100 calories per mile, so almost 5,000 calories total. I packed a mixture of different brands of gu packets, and some pita chips, cheese-itz, and Vienna fingers. For clothing, we carried a lightweight jacket, a rain coat, an emergency bivy, some medicine and such, gloves, and a beanie. Janna wore some running tights and I opted for my tried-and-true hiking pants. I think our clothing worked out great, but when I return, I think I'll just opt for a lightweight poncho. I needed my insulated jacket for about 20 minutes and I never even broke out the rain jacket. For a top layer, I wore a heavy baselayer and it worked out great. I hiked it in some nice Brooke's running shoes with flat soles. The shoes worked out great and I only got one minor blister—not bad for almost 50 miles.


We really cruised early on. We made it 10 miles before the sun rose on us somewhere past Phantom Ranch. On the north side of the river, the hiking was beautiful and easy. The trail is very kind all the way up to Supai where the elevation really kicks in. We got passed by probably 10 different people getting after the r2r2r before we reached the North Rim. We topped out on the North Rim at about 12:30PM, around 10 hours after we started. We did not go for speed on our way north becase we really wanted to conserve our energy (and joints) for the way back. We really should have had this strategy on the way back to Phantom Ranch as well, but the trail is so beautiful and mellow, we decided to jog. We made it back to the river before the sun escaped the canyon, sometime around 5:30PM. We caught a beautiful sunset from the bridge over the Colorado and then started up the final stretch. We made great time to Indian Gardens, but miles 44-48 we definitely the toughest. The last four miles gains around 3,500 ft. Normally, this is very manageable, but after a 40 mile day, it is a little rough. We pushed to the top though, and made it back to the car around 9:30PM. We made the journey in about 19 hours on the dot.


Overall, the hike was well worth it. It is hard to capture the scale of the Grand Canyon, even when you are staring at it. Walls that look so close are 5,000ft tall. The trail was lovely, if a little crowded. There were a lot of parties doing the r2r2r, and I am glad that so many people are able to experience so much of the Grand Canyon. The hike, while tough, was not quite as difficult as I had imagined it would be. After doing to much elevation in Colorado over the past few years, I was pretty accustomed to grinding out big days. The trail is beautiful and in wonderful shape. The "mental game" is not really an issue—if you ever get bored or down, just look at the incredible scenery all around you. It is an extremely enjoyable hike. Having a tough and fun partner makes all the difference, too. Thanks Janna!


Heading home



After getting off the trail, the first thing we wanted was hot, greasy, fatty food. We found the cafeteria open in the village and got some pretty bad pizza. It really is bad pizza if you still think it is bad after completing a 48 mile hike. The next morning we packed up, stopped for a quick picture, and made our way to Denver. We decided to drive the southern route through Durango on the way home. I had never been and the drive across Northeast Arizona was beautiful. We saw Monument Valley and Shiprock, both of which were incredible. We pulled into Denver at around 10:00PM, completing our marathon weekend. It was an awesome trip, one that I will definitely repeat!


Special thanks to Janna for completely planning this, convincing me to do it, and then convincing me that I could do it!
