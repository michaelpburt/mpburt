Climbing the Hill

September 6th, 2015




We had been talking about Rainier for over a year. Jacob, Chris, and myself had been doing a lot of climbing in Colorado, and we wanted a chance to put our skills to the test. Also, it had been almost 10 years to the day since I was last on a glacier. We set our date well in advance, and trained like hell all summer long. It never really feels like training in Colorado, its always more of a weekend adventure. But training it was. We were well prepared physically.


We flew into Seattle on Wednesday evening and immediately hit the sack. Jacob's friend, Chris T., was joining us for the adventure and graciously hosted us at his house. Thursday was mainly a prep day where we packed up our gear, made a stop at REI (because, why not?), and stocked up on food and other supplies. We then drove up to the park and camped out at a campsite very close to Paradise. Our plan was to practice some rope work and get a good night's rest. We got about 40 minutes of rope work in and then called it a night.


We got up quite early and we were at the Paradise trailhead around 6:00AM. The weather was shit in the morning, typical Seattle fog. We hoped it would break but it steadily grew throughout the day. This made for some fun and interesting route finding on the way up to Camp Muir, but nothing too serious.
